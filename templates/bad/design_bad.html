Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) Design Examples

Compare good vs bad design for better HCI understanding.

What is HCI?

Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) focuses on designing interfaces that enhance user experience by making applications easy to use, accessible, and efficient.

HCI Usability Principles

  • Visibility: The user should always know what is happening.
  • Feedback: Provide clear responses to user actions.
  • Efficiency: Interfaces should minimize user effort.
  • Consistency: Similar actions should have similar outcomes.

Good Design

A good design follows HCI principles such as clarity, consistency, and user-friendliness.

Bad Design

A bad design lacks usability principles, making navigation difficult and reducing efficiency.

Accessibility in HCI

HCI design should be inclusive, considering users with disabilities. Key factors include:

  • High color contrast for readability.
  • Keyboard navigation support.
  • Alternative text for images.

Comparison: Good vs. Bad Design

Criteria Good Design Bad Design
Navigation Easy and intuitive Confusing and cluttered
Readability Clear fonts and contrast Small fonts, poor contrast
Responsiveness Works on all devices Poor mobile compatibility
